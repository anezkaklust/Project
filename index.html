Studying

Once you come to London for university, studying will inevitably get a bit harder than what you have been used to at A-level or IB. Here are some frequent questions students have about the transition, and our answers with top tips for each of them!

  

What are the basic tips to stay organised at university?

Good question! We think that staying organised is the key to achieving good results. You will need to pay extra attention to synchronising your work across all your devices (Dropbox, OneDrive or Google Drive work really well for that), so that you don't find yourself in a situation when you need to work on an essay but don't have access to those changes you did last night on the library PC. Also, remember to synchronise your timetable with your phone's calendar. That way, you will always keep up to date with any changes, and avoid missing any lectures (of course, unless you don't manage to wake up, which just happens from time to time!). Make note of all the important deadlines and put them into your phone as well. Beware that not all deadlines are at midnight to avoid getting grade deductions for late submissions!

How to get ready for exams?

The most important thing is knowing what you will be assessed on! You can find this information in your module handbook, usually available from your university's online learning platform (Moodle). Some modules may not even require you to take any exam (lucky!), but just submit an essay, portfolio or other work. Make sure you know how you'll be assessed before the module starts, and modify your preparation accordingly.

What if I need help/don't understand a topic?

The academic staff are here to help you! All teachers will have office hours set aside for any queastions and queries, and they can help you go over the material again. Some teachers require that you book the office hour in advance, so always check your module handbook. Also, don't come to these sessions unprepared and expect the teacher to pour the knowledge in your head. Rather, you should study the material beforehand and prepare a list of questions that the teacher will help you answer!

Transport

London’s transport system might make your skin crawl at first. But trust us, it’s easy. We’ll explain to you in a simple way how exactly each form of public transport in London works.

Accomodation

London has numerous properties to offer, but there are even more people waiting to rent them. Competition is fierce and you need to be prepared, and know where to look in order to find something quickly. Note that London is quite an expensive place to live, and the rent is likely to be much higher than in your home country. Don’t despair; read our guide for some useful tips on how to find student accommodation in London.
